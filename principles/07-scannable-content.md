# Scannable Content

- Status: draft
- Domain: Shared
- Claim: A-13
- Family: composition
- Lens: typography
- Question: How should content be written for fast decision-making?
- Rule: Write for scanning before reading by using concise, objective, high-signal phrasing.
- Why it works: Users often decide what matters before they read full paragraphs.
- Use when: Listing cards, service summaries, dashboards, landing pages, and filter labels.
- Avoid when: Marketing filler hides the useful action, claim, or comparison detail.
- Small example: Replace vague value claims with a concrete outcome, audience, and time/cost effect.
